Cardamum

According to Ayurveda, Cardamom is tridoshic, which is good for balancing all three Doshas. It is excellent for balancing Kapha, particularly for the stomach and the lungs. It is also useful for pacifying Vata. However, people with intense Pitta should use Cardamom sparingly, as it is heating and can further aggravate it. Intake of black Cardamom helps in flushing out the toxins that could otherwise harm the skin and body. Chewing some black cardamom detoxifies the body, thus lending to clearer skin.

Native to South India, Cardamom was first cultivated for culinary and medicinal purposes. It is one of the most expensive spices in the world. It is a warming spice, enriched with Vitamin C, Calcium, Magnesium and Iron. With its sharp flavour, Cardamom is widely used in Indian cooking to enhance the aroma of both savoury and sweet dishes. It is considered beneficial for digestion, and helps reduce bloating.

How to use

It's powder in a dose of 250 - 500 mg can be taken along with ghee or honey. For bad breath problems, or in cases of diarrhea, cardamom is chewed or simply kept inside the mouth and the juice is swallowed slowly

Potential Benefits of Cardamom

  • Antioxidant.
  • Antimutagenic.
  • Antibacterial.
  • Anti-inflammatory.
  • Antidiabetic.
  • Heart protective.
  • Liver protective.

Who should avoid consuming Cardamom?

It is generally not recommended for pregnant women to consume Cardamom, as it can stimulate the uterus and cause uterine contractions. This could lead to a miscarriage, so it is best to avoid it during pregnancy.

Summary

Cardamom comes from the seeds of several different plants that belong to the same family as ginger. It has a distinctive flavor that complements both sweet and savory dishes. People may use cardamom seeds and pods in curries, desserts, and meat dishes, as well as in beverages, such as coffee and chai tea.
